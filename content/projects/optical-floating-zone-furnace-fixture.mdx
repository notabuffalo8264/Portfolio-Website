## Overview

Built a reusable fixture architecture for optical floating zone ampule growth that minimized manual alignment and improved cycle-to-cycle consistency.

## Problem / Constraints

- Tight thermal envelope and small handling clearances
- Frequent part swaps between experiments
- Existing fixture introduced misalignment drift

## Approach

Used datum-driven interfaces, indexed clamping features, and modular subcomponents to speed replacement while preserving repeatability.

<Callout title="Design Focus">
Prioritized mechanical repeatability and operator ergonomics equally, because setup quality and throughput were linked.
</Callout>
